# Open Source to Paid Models for OpenClaw-Adjacent Project

## Model Options

### 1. Open-Core Model
- Core product remains open source.
- Advanced features or enterprise add-ons released as proprietary software.
- Monetization avenues: premium features licensing, professional support, consulting, and/or cloud hosting.

### 2. Dual Licensing
- Codebase available under an open source license and a separate commercial license.
- Monetization: sell commercial licenses to customers seeking warranty, legal indemnity, or non-GPL licensing.

### 3. Hosted Offering / SaaS
- Software available for free self-hosting or as a paid hosted SaaS.
- Monetization: subscription fees for hosted service, freemium usage limits, premium support tiers.

## Lightweight Roadmap

- **Phase 1:** Develop core open source product; build a community; release under permissive license.
- **Phase 2:** Identify and build premium, proprietary features or modules.
- **Phase 3:** Implement a dual licensing scheme for enterprise customers.
- **Phase 4:** Launch a hosted SaaS offering with tiered subscription plans.
- **Phase 5:** Expand into professional services including support, consulting, and customization.

## Monetization Mechanics

- Licensing premium features as add-ons.
- Offering commercial enterprise licenses.
- SaaS subscription plans: freemium + paid tiers.
- Professional support contracts.
- Consulting and custom development services.

This approach balances community engagement with monetization potential, leveraging open source benefits while generating sustainable revenue streams.
